Act like a leader – No title required.
Practical actions to build leadership presence at every career stage
How to use this Pick 1–2 boxes to try this week. Leadership is built through small, visible actions.
Early Mid Senior
Ownership & Initiative
Move things forward without being asked
 Ask: "Is there anything blocking this?" and take on one small fix without waiting Identify a recurring problem and propose a simple solution in your team channel Take ownership of a vague problem space and define what success looks like
Decision-Making & Momentum
Reduce ambiguity and keep things moving
 When stuck, suggest 1 option and ask for feedback instead of waiting Present 2–3 options with trade-offs and recommend one Make a call in ambiguity and clearly communicate why and next steps
Communication & Influence
Be clear and bring others along
 Before speaking, clarify your "ask" (question, decision, or update) Tailor your message to your audience (tech vs non-tech) in meetings Align stakeholders by framing decisions in terms of impact and priorities
Product & Impact Thinking
Understand why the work matters
 Ask "Who is this for?" and "What problem are we solving?" Connect your work to user or business outcomes when giving updates Challenge work that doesn't clearly tie to impact or strategy
Elevating Others
Make the team stronger
 Acknowledge a teammate's contribution in a meeting or chat Create space by inviting quieter voices into discussions Actively sponsor someone by advocating for their work in the right rooms
Self-Leadership & Visibility
Make your work and growth visible
 Share a weekly update on what you've learned or shipped Ask for specific feedback on one skill you're developing Publicly highlight team wins and articulate your team's impact to leadership

Quick Self-Audit

  • Which area feels easiest right now?
  • Which area are you avoiding?
  • What is one action you will try this week?

Keep Going

Leadership isn't a promotion — it's a practice. Every time you take ownership, communicate with clarity, or lift someone else up, you're leading. Start small. Stay consistent. It compounds.
